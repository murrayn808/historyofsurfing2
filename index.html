A history of surfing

Introduction

Surfing, often regarded as one of the world's most iconic water sports, has a rich history that traces back centuries. It originated in Polynesian cultures, where it was an integral part of daily life. Ancient Hawaiians, in particular, developed and perfected the art of riding waves on wooden boards, an activity they called "he ªe nalu." Surfing held deep cultural and spiritual significance in Hawaiian society, with social status and even nobility associated with expertise in the sport. However, surfing began to decline during European colonization and missionary influence in Hawaii. It experienced a resurgence in the early 20th century, thanks to pioneering surfers like Duke Kahanamoku, who introduced the sport to the world. Today, surfing is a global phenomenon, with a thriving culture, professional competitions, and a profound impact on art, fashion, and lifestyle around the world.
